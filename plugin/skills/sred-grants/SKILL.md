# SR&ED & Government Grants

Help Canadian small business owners understand SR&ED tax credits and available government grant programs. SR&ED claims should always involve a qualified SR&ED consultant or tax advisor — this skill helps owners assess eligibility and prepare documentation.

## SR&ED Program Overview

Canada's Scientific Research & Experimental Development (SR&ED) program delivers ~$3–4B annually to businesses. It is the **highest-value tax program** for innovative companies.

**Two components:**
1. **Full deduction** of SR&ED expenditures in the year incurred (or carry forward indefinitely)
2. **Investment Tax Credit (ITC)** — a % of eligible expenditures credited against tax owing

## ITC Rates

| Business Type | ITC Rate | Refundable? |
|---|---|---|
| CCPC — first $3M of eligible expenditures | **35%** | **Yes — fully refundable** |
| CCPC — above $3M expenditure limit | 15% | No |
| Non-CCPC / large corporation | 15% | No |

**The 35% refundable ITC is the most powerful part:** a CCPC spending $100K on qualifying SR&ED gets $35K back as a cash refund — even if no tax is owed.

CCPC's $3M expenditure limit phases out as taxable capital grows between $10M and $50M.

## The Three-Part Eligibility Test

Work qualifies for SR&ED only if it meets **all three criteria:**

**1. Scientific or Technological Uncertainty**
There was a genuine uncertainty that existing publicly available knowledge couldn't resolve. The challenge was not obvious. The uncertainty must exist *before* the work begins — not discovered mid-project.

**2. Scientific or Technological Advancement**
The work generated new knowledge in science or technology. Business advancement alone (e.g., "we implemented a known algorithm in our product") does not qualify. The knowledge gained must advance the field, even if only incrementally.

**3. Systematic Investigation**
Work was conducted through formulating hypotheses, testing, observing results, and drawing conclusions. Must be structured — not random trial-and-error or ad hoc troubleshooting.

## What Does NOT Qualify

- Commercial production or deployment (once the solution is known, work stops qualifying)
- Routine data collection or analysis
- Market research, customer surveys, usability testing
- Style changes, cosmetic modifications
- Social science, arts, or humanities research
- Engineering work that applies established techniques
- Prospecting for minerals

## Eligible Expenditures

| Category | Eligible | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salaries & wages of SR&ED employees | Yes | 80% for employees spending < 90% of time on SR&ED |
| Subcontractor costs (arm's-length) | Yes | 80% of amount paid |
| Materials consumed/transformed | Yes | |
| Overhead — Proxy Method | Yes | 55% of eligible salaries |
| Capital expenditures | No | Eliminated in 2014 |
| Management fees | No | |

**Most small businesses use the Proxy Method** for overhead — simpler than tracking actual overhead.

## Filing Requirements

- **Form T661** filed with the T2 return
- **Deadline:** 12 months after the T2 filing due date = 18 months after fiscal year-end
- **Documentation required:** CRA expects contemporaneous records (created during the work, not reconstructed)

**Essential documentation:**
- Technical descriptions of the uncertainty and hypotheses
- Lab notebooks, design documents, architecture diagrams
- Test plans, test results, iteration logs
- Time sheets showing SR&ED hours
- Meeting minutes discussing technical challenges
- Code revision history (Git logs count)

⚠️ **Reconstructed records are a major CRA review risk.** Document as you go.

## Federal Grant Programs for Small Businesses (2026)

| Program | Funder | What It Funds | Typical Amount |
|---|---|---|---|
| IRAP (Industrial Research Assistance Program) | NRC | R&D salaries, commercialization | $50K–$500K+ |
| Canada Job Grant | ESDC / provinces | Employee training and upskilling | Up to 2/3 of training costs |
| Women Entrepreneurship Strategy (WES) | ISED | Women-owned businesses | Various streams |
| CanExport SMEs | Trade Commissioner | Export market development | Up to $50K (50% cost-share) |
| SIF (Strategic Innovation Fund) | ISED | Large-scale innovation projects | $10M+ (large companies) |
| BDC Venture Capital / Loans | BDC | Growth financing | Various |

> ⚠️ **CDAP (Canada Digital Adoption Program) was wound down in 2024.** The Boost Your Business Technology grant and Digital Advisor stream are no longer accepting applications. Check canada.ca or contact BDC/ISED for any successor digital adoption programs.

**Provincial programs (examples):**
- Ontario: OCE (Ontario Centres of Excellence), Invest Ottawa
- BC: Innovate BC, CleanBC programs
- Alberta: Alberta Innovates
- Quebec: Investissement Québec, PROMPT

## SR&ED vs. IRAP — Key Differences

| | SR&ED | IRAP |
|---|---|---|
| Type | Tax credit (claim after year-end) | Grant (paid during project) |
| Cash timing | After filing T2 | Quarterly during project |
| Application required | No — just file T661 | Yes — competitive |
| Refundable for CCPCs | Yes (35%) | N/A (it's a grant) |
| Best for | Any qualifying R&D | Early-stage / growing companies |

Many companies claim **both SR&ED and IRAP** on the same project — they are stackable (with some restrictions).
